Trait Reference

Batter Traits

Contact Hitters (C+) — On our d100 table: any roll landing in the 1B+ chip range → batter doubles and runners advance 2 bases; do not roll for defense. Bunting is automatically successful. Contact Hitters always convert sacrifice flies (unless the fielder catching it is a Great Defender).

Free Swingers (C-) — On our d100 table: a roll landing in the top 40% of the 1B/ADV range is a strikeout instead of a single (this corresponds to d20 rolls 11-12, the top 2 of the 5 slots in that range). Bunt attempts succeed only on a d20 roll of 1-2. Free Swingers can't hit sac flies. Can be shifted against.

S+ Speedy Runners — On our d100 table, the 1B+ chip represents 2 d20 slots. Split it in half: a roll in the lower half of 1B+ → player doubles, runners advance 2 (no DEF). A roll in the upper half of 1B+ → player triples (no DEF). Add 1 to the d8 steal roll for 2nd/3rd. Can steal home (roll of 8 on a d8, runner on 3rd). Can bunt for a hit: a lower-half 1B+ roll when bunting is treated as 1B/3B instead; other bunt results follow the bunting table. Can attempt a bunt for a hit any time, even with no runners on base. Can't be doubled up — the lead runner is out but the Speedy Runner reaches first safely, unless the fielder starting the double play is a Great Defender.

S- Slow Runners — Cannot hit triples: any d100 roll landing in the 3B/D range is treated as 2B/ADV instead. Subtract 3 from every stolen base roll. Easier to double up — any ground ball to the infield is a double play regardless of MSS.

D+ Great Defenders — Add 1 to any DEF roll by this player. With runners on base, a modified DEF roll of 13 means the lead runner is out and the batter reaches safely. If the Great Defender is a catcher, the starting pitcher begins with an extra Pitch Die level (max d12; bonus doesn't apply to relievers). Can turn a routine grounder into a double play: in a double-play situation with MSS < 70, a DEF roll of 10-12 turns it. Can throw out a runner at the plate on a would-be sac fly with a DEF roll of 10-12 (the runner may instead hold at third to avoid the challenge).

D- Poor Defenders — Subtract 1 from any DEF roll. If a catcher, any pitcher (including relievers) starts one Pitch Die level lower. Before starting a double play, must roll DEF first — a 1 or 2 means the ball is thrown away and all runners are safe. In a sac fly situation, a runner at third always scores on a ball hit to a D- outfielder, regardless of MSS. Any player playing out of position counts as a Poor Defender.

Pitcher Traits

A pitcher can be a Strikeout Artist (K+), a Groundball Machine (GB+), or a Control Pitcher (CN+), or none of those — plus any pitcher can separately have Great Stamina (ST+).

K+ Strikeout Artist — Add 1 to every MSS. A result of 3 on the Out Table (normally a grounder to first) is a strikeout instead.

GB+ Groundball Machine — Add 1 to every MSS. A result of 2 on the Out Table (normally a strikeout) is a 6-3 grounder to short instead.

CN+ Control Pitcher — Only gives up a walk when MSS is 1-2 points greater than the BT.

ST+ Great Stamina — Starts losing Pitch Die levels for innings pitched past the seventh instead of the sixth.

P- All pitchers bat as P- by default — subtract 1 from any Hit Die roll when batting.

Pitcher Fatigue

Starters drop a Pitch Die level if they: allow 3+ runs in an inning; allow 4+ runs over two innings; for every run allowed over 4; for every inning pitched past six.

Relief pitchers lose a Pitch Die level for every run allowed, and if they pitch more than one inning.

All pitchers gain a Pitch Die level if they: strike out the side; put up three straight shutout innings; or escape a bases-loaded, no-out jam without allowing a run.
